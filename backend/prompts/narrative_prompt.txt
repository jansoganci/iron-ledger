PART 2 — RECONCILIATION FINDINGS (only when reconciliations list is non-empty)

For each reconciliation item, write a structured finding using ONLY the classification taxonomy below.

Classification taxonomy — pick the ONE that best fits based on the hints provided:

1. timing_cutoff
   When to use: delta amount falls in a period near month-end, or the item crosses a period boundary.
   Template: "The [account] shows a [delta] difference between [source A] ([amount A]) and the GL ([GL amount]). This appears to be a timing cut-off: the transaction was recorded in [source] before the accounting period closed. No action required — expect this to clear next month."

2. categorical_misclassification
   When to use: amounts are similar but booked in different GL lines (e.g., bonus in Contractors vs Payroll), or one source has more granular line items that sum to the same total elsewhere.
   Template: "The [account] shows a [delta] gap between [source A] ([amount A]) and the GL ([GL amount]). The GL appears to have booked this amount under a different category. Recommended action: review the GL coding and reclassify to align with [source A]."

3. missing_je
   When to use: item appears in one source only (is_source_only or is_gl_only hint is true), or delta is equal to a single invoice/line item amount.
   Template: "The [account] shows [amount] in [source] with no matching entry in the GL. A journal entry may be missing. Recommended action: verify whether this transaction has been posted and enter the missing JE if not."

4. stale_reference
   When to use: a reference list (contracts, price list, customer roster) does not match the GL — customer count or rate differences.
   Template: "The [account] reference list shows [amount] but the GL records [GL amount], a [delta] gap. The reference data may be out of date. Recommended action: reconcile the active list against the GL and update cancelled or re-priced items."

5. accrual_mismatch
   When to use: a large lump-sum invoice hit that should be spread monthly (e.g., annual software subscription).
   Template: "The [account] GL shows a lump-sum of [GL amount] where the monthly cost should be approximately [expected monthly]. This appears to be an accrual timing issue — the full invoice was expensed rather than amortized. Recommended action: create a prepaid asset and amortize over the contract period."

6. structural_explained
   When to use: the delta is fully explained by fees, platform deductions, or structural netting that doesn't represent an error.
   Template: "The [account] shows a [delta] difference between sources. This is structurally explained by [reason — e.g., platform fees deducted before deposit, netting of refunds]. No action required."
